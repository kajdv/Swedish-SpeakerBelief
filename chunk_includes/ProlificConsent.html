Project Title: Language Comprehension in Context

CONSENT FORM: Comprehension in context and its time course

The present research is being conducted by Florian Schwarz from the Department of Linguistics at the University of Pennsylvania.

Purpose: The purpose of this study is to understand the way in which humans comprehend language in context. Because of the nature of this study, specific hypotheses and predictions cannot be divulged until after the experiment, at which time a detailed debriefing will be provided.

Selection of Participants: Participants in this experiment are normal adult human beings, who voluntarily agree to participate through electronic postings on https://upenn.sona-systems.com. Participants should be native speakers of English.

Procedure: The experiment instructions will include a detailed explanation of the specific procedure used in the present experiment. Generally speaking, we are interested in understanding how you comprehend language. We will ask you to perform simple language-based tasks involving language, such as reading sentences and answering questions about them.

Risks: This study has no known risks associated with it.

Alternatives: Since participation in the study is completely voluntary, the alternative is to not participate in the study. You can decide not to participate in the experiment.

Compensation: You will receive extra course credit for participation in the experiment, for the eligible course specified when signing up in the subject pool via https://upenn.sona-systems.com. Participation in this study also offers the opportunity to learn something about how psycholinguistic research is conducted.

Withdrawal: Your participation in this study is completely voluntary and you may withdraw at any time without prejudicing any present or future connection that you might have to the University of Pennsylvania. You are encouraged to contact the experimenter with any questions you may have regarding any topic discussed in this consent form.

Confidentiality: You understand that every attempt will be made by the investigators to maintain all information collected in this study as strictly confidential, except as may be required by court order or by law. Authorized representatives of the University of Pennsylvania Institutional Review Board (IRB), a committee charged with protecting the rights and welfare of research subjects, may be provided access to research records that identify you by name. If any publications or presentations result from this research, you will not be identified by name.

I have read and understood the above information and by completing the survey, I express my consent to participating in the study.
